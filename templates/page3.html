About

Alignment to the UN Sustainability Goals

Our mission is to address the global issue of food waste, which closely aligns with the UN's Sustainable Development Goal 12. This goal emphasizes responsible consumption and production. Food production consumes precious resources like energy and water. Unfortunately, when we waste food, these resources are squandered. Additionally, transporting food via fossil fuel-powered vehicles contributes to harmful carbon emissions. By reducing food waste, we can significantly curb carbon dioxide emissions.

To put it into perspective, the UN's Food and Agriculture Organization reports that food waste accounts for nearly ten percent of global greenhouse gas emissions. In some regions, wasted food ends up in landfills, where it decomposes and produces methane gas, a potent heat-trapping greenhouse gas, according to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. Hence, food waste is a pressing international problem.

Our project aims to combat these challenges by empowering households to make efficient use of ingredients they already have for their everyday meals. In doing so, we strive to contribute to the following UN subgoals:

  • 12.3: "By 2030, halve per capita global food waste at the retail and consumer levels and reduce food losses along production and supply chains, including post-harvest losses."
    Our project actively promotes responsible food consumption and helps households use their ingredients more efficiently, thereby working towards this subgoal.
  • 12.5: "By 2030, substantially reduce waste generation through prevention, reduction, recycling, and reuse."
    We also contribute to this subgoal by placing a strong emphasis on waste reduction and encouraging the efficient use of resources.
